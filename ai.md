# AI & Technology Leadership Resources

A curated collection of resources exploring the intersection of AI, technology, and leadership in the modern era.

## People to Follow

- **Wade Chambers** - Chief Engineering Officer @Amplitude_HQ

  Chambers is one of the more visible engineering executives publicly working through what it means to build AI-native product organizations rather than bolt AI onto existing processes. His posts and talks are grounded in the operational realities of a mid-scale SaaS company, not abstract theory. For engineering leaders, he's valuable precisely because he's navigating the same tension most face: extracting durable competitive advantage from AI tooling before it becomes commoditized. His candor about what hasn't worked is rarer than his peers.

## Books

### Read
- **[The Scaling Era: An Oral History of AI, 2019–2025](https://press.stripe.com/scaling)** - Dwarkesh Patel with Gavin Leech

  This oral history reconstructs the key decisions, false starts, and emergent dynamics of the scaling hypothesis era through primary sources — the people who were actually in the rooms. The engineering leader benefit is epistemic: understanding which bets were obvious in hindsight but genuinely uncertain at the time calibrates better judgment for the next wave of ambiguous platform shifts. The non-obvious takeaway is how much of the "inevitable" progress was contingent on a handful of infrastructure and compute procurement decisions that could easily have gone differently.

## Podcasts

- **[The AI Native Dev](https://ainativedev.io/)** - From Copilot today to AI Native Software Tomorrow

  This podcast tracks the transition from AI-assisted coding (autocomplete, Copilot) toward fully AI-native software development workflows, with guests who are actively building or managing teams through that shift. For engineering leaders, it's a useful pulse on where the productivity frontier actually is versus where vendor marketing claims it is. A recurring non-obvious theme is how AI-native development changes the skill composition of effective engineering teams — not just individual productivity but what you hire for and how you structure review and ownership.

- **[How I AI](https://open.spotify.com/show/4aRP2XSavdtrLG5FZoonOK)** - Claire Vo

  Claire Vo, a CPO, interviews practitioners about their personal AI workflows with an emphasis on product and leadership roles rather than pure engineering. The value for engineering leaders is that it surfaces how your product and design counterparts are adopting AI, which shapes cross-functional collaboration dynamics you'll need to navigate. The non-obvious angle is that many guests describe AI as changing *what problems they choose to work on* rather than just how fast they work — a reframing with real implications for roadmap and prioritization conversations.

- **[Acquired AI](https://open.spotify.com/show/6xgOxvAluaTgEtnJ13AJN1)** - Deep dives into AI companies and strategies

  This series applies the Acquired model — exhaustive company history, business model anatomy, and competitive dynamics — to AI-era companies and strategic moves. Engineering leaders benefit because understanding the business and architectural decisions that created durable advantages (or didn't) at Nvidia, OpenAI, Anthropic, and others sharpens how to evaluate platform bets internally. The non-obvious payoff is developing a vocabulary for talking to your board and exec team about AI strategy that goes beyond capability benchmarks.

## Videos

### AI Strategy & Leadership
- **[Amplitude's Strategy for Building a Sustainable AI Advantage](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nxLDxizgilI)** - Wade Chambers

  Chambers lays out how Amplitude approached the question of whether AI is a feature, a platform, or a structural shift in the analytics market — and what that diagnosis implies for engineering investment and organization. The talk is unusually specific about the internal decision process, including where initial assumptions were wrong, which makes it more useful than most "AI strategy" presentations. For engineering leaders, the concrete takeaway is a framework for distinguishing AI capabilities that create lock-in versus those that competitors will commoditize within 12–18 months.

- **[Leadership in the Age of AI](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CWEWBgVwFc8)** - Paul Hudson and Lindsay Levin | TED

  This TED talk examines how AI is reshaping the nature of organizational decision-making and what that demands from leaders who were trained in pre-AI management paradigms. Hudson and Levin argue that the leadership skills most at risk are not technical — they're the judgment, narrative, and trust-building capabilities that AI cannot replicate but that leaders often underinvest in during technology transitions. The non-obvious provocation is that leaders who over-index on understanding AI technically may be optimizing for the wrong edge, while those who sharpen human-system judgment have a more durable advantage.
